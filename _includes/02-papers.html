  • Beyond directed hypergraphs: heterogeneous hypergraphs and spectral centralities
    R. Criado, M. Romance
    We provide an alternative definition of non-undirectedness (i.e. heterogeneity) in hypergraphs, beyond traditional definitions of directed hypergraphs which rely on set-theoretic notions. We discuss different examples of heterogeneous hypergraphs, focusing on their spectral centralities.

  • Uplifting edges in higher order networks: spectral centralities for non-uniform hypergraphs
    C. Pérez-Corral, M. Romance
    We provide a way to compute spectral centralities of general hypergraphs (in particular, the H-eigenvector centrality). Up until this work there was a major shortcoming, the requirement of the hypergraph to be uniform. We show that suitably adding an extra node does the trick.
    Preprint at arXiv:2310.20335
  • The transition to synchronization of networked systems
    A. Bayani, F. Nazarimehr, S. Jafari, K. Kovalenko, K. Alfaro-Bittner, R. J. Sánchez-García, S. Boccaletti
    In this work, the path to synchronization is fully characterized, in the sense that we find a way to predict analytically which clusters can each node belong to, and furthermore we obtain the value of the coupling at which each cluster synchronizes.
    Nature Communications

  • Detecting communities in higher-order networks by using their derivative graphs
    R. Criado, G. Vera de Salas, J. Yang
    Based on the concept of the derivative of a hypergraph, which serves as a similarity index akin to Jaccard's one in set theory, we devise a novel way to detect communities in undirected hypergraphs.
    Chaos, Solitons and Fractals
    Vol 177, 114200 (2023)

  • A compartmental model for cyber-epidemics
    D. Aleja, K. Alfaro-Bittner, E. Primo, R. Criado, M. Romance, S. Boccaletti
    We attempted to model the effect (in terms of damage) of the spread of a computer virus through a network, in terms of a vectorial compartmental model. The dependence of the final damage on the network's topology is discussed, as well as different viral behaviors.
